Weight Dequantization Hoisting

Serving massive LLMs requires quantizing weights to 4-bit (INT4). However, GPUs perform math in FP16 or BF16. Symbolic "Dequantization Hoisting" allows the engine to pull dequantization scales out of the inner GEMM loop, enabling high-speed integer math with floating-point precision.

The Problem

Loop-Level Dequantization Overhead

In a quantized Linear layer (W4A16), the weights W are stored as 4-bit integers plus a scale factor S. The math is:
Y = X × (W_int × S)

If the GPU dequantizes every weight before the multiplication inside the dot-product loop, it must perform a floating-point multiplication for every single element. This negates the speed benefit of using 4-bit weights and increases register pressure.

Symbolic Insight

Hoisting Scales out of GEMM

A symbolic optimizer identifies that the scale S is constant across a block of weights (e.g., a group of 128). Using the linearity of the matrix product, it hoists the scaling operation:
X × (W_int × S) → (X × W_int) × S

This transformation allows the hardware to:

  • INT Math: Perform the bulk of the dot-product using fast integer instructions (or INT8 Tensor Cores).
  • Post-Scaling: Apply the floating-point scale S only once to the final accumulated result.

This "hoisting" is what enables W4A16 kernels (like those in AutoGPTQ or Marlin) to outperform standard FP16 kernels by up to 2x.

Why It Matters

Large Language Model Quantization

Techniques like AWQ and GPTQ rely on this symbolic hoisting. By moving the dequantization math out of the hot-path, we maximize the compute-to-bandwidth ratio.

This is the difference between an 8-bit model that runs at the same speed as a 16-bit model, and one that runs twice as fast.
