Log-Expansion for Numerical Stability

In machine learning, we often multiply long chains of probabilities. This leads to catastrophic floating-point underflow. Symbolic log-expansion converts these products into sums, dramatically improving numerical stability and precision.

The Problem

The Underflow Bottleneck

Probabilities are values in [0, 1]. Multiplying 100 small probabilities (e.g., 0.01100) quickly exceeds the capacity of standard 64-bit floats, resulting in a "zero" that destroys gradients during backpropagation.

This is why we work in Log-Space. But transforming a complex mathematical model into its log-equivalent manually is error-prone.

Symbolic Insight

The Log-Expansion Cascade

A symbolic engine uses three fundamental laws of logarithms to expand a product into a sum:

  • LogProduct: log(x × y) → log(x) + log(y)
  • LogPower: log(xk) → k × log(x)
  • LogQuotient: log(x / y) → log(x) - log(y)

By applying these rules, log( (P1 × P2) / P3k ) becomes log(P1) + log(P2) - k × log(P3), which is numerically robust.

Why It Matters

Cross-Entropy and Log-Sum-Exp

This expansion is the foundation of the Cross-Entropy Loss function and the Log-Sum-Exp trick. Without symbolic expansion, the gradients of these functions would vanish or explode.

By automating the expansion, researchers can define high-level probabilistic models without worrying about the low-level numerical implementation details.
