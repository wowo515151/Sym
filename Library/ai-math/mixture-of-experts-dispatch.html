Mixture of Experts (MoE) Dispatch

MoE models like Mixtral and DeepSeek achieve massive parameter counts with relatively low compute by only activating a subset of "experts" for each token. Optimizing the dispatch and execution of these experts is a symbolic challenge involving dynamic stacking and gated MatMuls.

The Problem

Sparse Computation, Dense Latency

In an MoE layer, a Gating Network decides which expert Ei processes which token. Mathematically:
Result = Σ Gating(X)i × Experti(X)

If implemented naively as individual matrix multiplications for each token-expert pair, the GPU occupancy drops significantly. The overhead of many small kernels stalls the pipeline.

Symbolic Insight

Expert Stacking and Shared Sinks

Symbolic engines identify that the experts share the same structural form, differing only in their weight values. This allows for **Expert Stacking**:

  • Identify Commonality: Recognize that all Experti paths are MatMul(X, Wi).
  • Symbolic Rewrite: Transform the sum of gated products into a single Grouped MatMul or Stacked Kernel.

By rewriting the graph to use GroupedMatMul(X, StackedWeights, GatingIndices), we move from N small kernels to 1 unified kernel that handles the entire MoE block in a single pass.

Why It Matters

Throughput for Large Scale Models

The efficiency of MoE depends entirely on the Dispatch/Combine overhead. Symbolic optimization ensures that the router's decisions are translated into hardware-friendly batch operations.

This is critical for models like Llama-3-MoE or DeepSeek-V3, where the routing logic must be fused with the expert computation to minimize GPU stalls and synchronize expert outputs efficiently.
