Trigonometric Sum-to-Product Transformations

Sum-to-product identities are the secret weapon of signal processing. By converting the addition of two waves into a product, we can identify beat frequencies, modulations, and interference patterns that are hidden in the sum.

The Problem

The Interference of Waves

When two sound waves or electromagnetic signals overlap, they combine via linear superposition: sin(A) + sin(B). While this is mathematically simple, it is physically opaque.

A product form, like 2 sin((A+B)/2) cos((A-B)/2), reveals the "average" frequency and the "envelope" or "beat" frequency, which describes the physical behavior of the interference.

Symbolic Insight

Symmetry in the Complex Plane

Symbolic engines derive these identities by representing trigonometric functions as exponentials in the complex plane (Euler's formula).

The addition eiA + eiB can be factored by pulling out the average phase, leading directly to the product form.

  • Input: sin(A) + sin(B)
  • Identity: 2 sin((A+B)/2) × cos((A-B)/2)
Why It Matters

Beat Frequencies and FM Radio

In Signal Analysis, the sum-to-product identity is used to calculate the bandwidth of a modulated signal. In Acoustics, it explains why two slightly out-of-tune instruments create a "wobbling" sound.

For a symbolic computation engine, these identities are crucial for Integrand Normalization. Many integrals that look impossible in sum-form become trivial when converted to a product of trig terms.
