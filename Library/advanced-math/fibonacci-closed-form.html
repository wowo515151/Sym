The Fibonacci Sequence: Binet's Formula

The Fibonacci sequence is defined by a simple recurrence: Fn = Fn-1 + Fn-2. However, to compute the n-th Fibonacci number efficiently, we use Binet's formula, which provides a direct closed-form expression in terms of the Golden Ratio (φ).

The Problem

The Cost of Recurrence

A naive recursive implementation of Fibonacci has exponential time complexity O(2n). While memoization or dynamic programming reduces this to O(n), a closed-form symbolic expression allows us to compute Fn in constant time O(1).

Deriving this formula involves solving the characteristic equation of the linear recurrence relation.

Symbolic Insight

Solving the Characteristic Equation

The recurrence Fn - Fn-1 - Fn-2 = 0 has a characteristic polynomial:
r2 - r - 1 = 0

Using the quadratic formula, a symbolic engine finds the roots:

  • r1 = (1 + √5) / 2 (φ)
  • r2 = (1 - √5) / 2 (ψ)

Binet's formula is then: Fn = (φn - ψn) / √5.

Why It Matters

The Golden Ratio in Nature and Computing

Binet's formula is the ultimate example of how irrational numbers (√5) can combine to form a sequence of pure integers.

In computer science, this formula is used to analyze the complexity of the Euclidean algorithm and is a prime case study for the Eigenvalue method of solving systems of difference equations.
